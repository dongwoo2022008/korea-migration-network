# 대한민국 시군구 인구이동 네트워크의 진화와 지역 흡인력 분석

## 2006~2025 전국 시군구 패널데이터를 중심으로

---

**Abstract**

This study investigates the structural evolution of the interregional migration network in South Korea and identifies the determinants of regional attractiveness using panel data of 229 municipalities from 2006 to 2025 (4,122 observations). We employ a tripartite methodology: directed weighted network analysis, the Spatial Durbin Model (SDM), and an XGBoost-based machine learning approach combined with SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations). The empirical results yield three major findings. First, the migration network exhibits an entrenched polarization, where the top 20 hub regions consistently monopolize approximately 28-30% of the total in-migration flows. The Jaccard similarity index (declining to 0.43 by 2025) indicates a significant generational shift in these hubs from traditional urban centers to emerging planned cities. Second, the SDM estimations quantitatively confirm the existence of a straw effect (spatial spillover); an increase in a region's network status (PageRank) significantly boosts its own net migration rate (direct effect) while simultaneously draining population from adjacent regions (indirect effect). Third, the SHAP analysis reveals that PageRank is the most critical determinant of regional attractiveness (mean absolute SHAP value = 14.38), overwhelmingly surpassing traditional socio-economic and infrastructure indicators.

**Keywords:** Migration Network, Regional Attractiveness, PageRank, Spatial Durbin Model, SHAP, Local Extinction
